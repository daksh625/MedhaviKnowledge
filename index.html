About Medhavi Skills University

Medhavi Skills University (MSU) is India's first private Skills University, established under the Medhavi Skills University Act 2021, Govt. of Chhattisgarh. UGC-recognised, NAAC-accredited, ISO 9001:2015 certified. Tagline: Skill · Empower · Liberate.

Design Philosophy

Captured in the phrase PERFORM · EXPERIENCE · EXPLORE · CREATE — the four learning verbs of a Skills University graduate — versus the traditional university's UNDERSTAND & MEMORISE. Employers co-design, co-deliver, assess, and fund programmes. Industry is not a downstream recipient — it is the primary architect.

Mission

To bridge the gap between education and employment by embedding learning inside industry — creating job-ready graduates who contribute from Day 1.

Vision

To be India's largest work-integrated university ecosystem, globally recognised for producing skilled, certified, and degree-qualified industry professionals.

Direct (Corporate)

MSU partners directly with a corporate to deliver workforce skilling, credentialing, and degree programmes for their employees. Custom QPs designed around company SOPs. MSU + MAPL execute jointly — MSU provides degrees, MAPL handles NAPS/payroll.

NAPS Explainer — How It Works & Savings

What is NAPS?

National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) is a Central Government initiative under the Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship. It incentivises employers to engage apprentices by reimbursing 25% of the prescribed stipend (maximum Rs. 1,500 per apprentice per month) directly to the employer.

How NAPS Works (5 Steps)

1. Employer registers on apprenticeshipindia.gov.in portal
2. Apprentice engaged via tripartite agreement (Employer + MSU + Apprentice)
3. Apprentice registered on NAPS portal; Aadhaar-linked
4. Monthly stipend paid by employer; GoI reimburses 25% (up to Rs. 1,500/month) quarterly
5. On completion: NCVET/MSU certificate issued; apprentice converted to employee or exits with credential

EP — Education Partnerships (B2B)

EP is MSU's model for external training institutes, skill centres, coaching institutes, and educational bodies seeking UGC-recognised credentials without building university infrastructure. Two sub-types: Sectoral (60+ partners) and Awarding Body (141 active partners).

EP Partnership Tiers

Awarding Body EP (141 Partners)

Who qualifies: Any registered training institute, ITI, coaching centre, EdTech platform, skill development centre, or vocational institute with an existing learner base.

What they offer: Any sector — IT, BFSI, healthcare, vocational, etc. Broad-based curriculum delivery.

What MSU provides: UGC-recognised degree or NHEQF certificate affiliation, academic oversight, assessment standards, LMS access, DigiLocker credential issuance, co-branding.

Revenue model: EP earns programme fees from learners. MSU earns per-learner affiliation fee. No upfront franchise cost for most models.

Sectoral EP (60+ Partners)

Who qualifies: Domain-specialist institutes — medical colleges, law schools, fintech academies, beauty institutes, gaming studios, aviation academies, Ayurvedic institutions.

What they offer: Deep sector-specific curriculum in one domain (e.g., ORANE for beauty, GJA for gems, IMS Proschool for BFSI, Gamer2Maker for gaming).

What MSU provides: Sector-specific curriculum co-design support, NHEQF mapping, NCVET alignment, sector employer connection via IP network, specialised credential design.

Revenue model: Similar to Awarding Body + potential revenue-share on placement outcomes and sector-specific branding premium.

What EP Partners Get

EP learners receive degrees/certificates issued by MSU — a UGC Section 2(f) & 12(B) recognised university. Credentials issued via DigiLocker, linked to APAAR/ABC. Fully portable and verifiable.

NHEQF Course Catalogue

Access to MSU's 240+ NHEQF course titles. EPs can offer any course from the catalogue without developing their own curriculum. MSU provides course content framework; EP delivers training.

Technology Stack

LMS with co-branding, APAAR/ABC integration, DigiLocker issuance, online proctored assessment, student progress dashboards, mobile-first interface for learners.

Marketing & Sales Support

Co-branded materials, MSU listing and website presence, UGC documentation for parent/learner conversations, joint digital campaigns, lead sharing from MSU's central marketing.

Placement Network

Access to MSU's 300+ employer connections via IP vertical. Job fairs, campus hiring events, employer-specific batch hiring programmes for top EP partners.

Faculty Development

MSU academic team provides EP faculty orientation, curriculum alignment workshops, assessment calibration sessions, and continuous faculty development support.

What is a Custom QP and Why Does It Matter?

What is a Qualification Pack (QP)?

A QP is the foundational document that defines a job role — it specifies the National Occupational Standards (NOS), performance criteria, knowledge requirements, skill elements, assessment strategies, and credit value for a specific occupation at a specific NCrF/NSQF level.

QPs are the building blocks of all NSQF-aligned training. Without a QP, a programme cannot be NSQF-mapped, NAPS-eligible, or ABC-credited. All MSU programmes are built on QPs — either standard SSC QPs or MSU custom QPs.

NCrF AlignedNSQF MappedNAPS EligibleABC Credits

Why Custom vs. Standard SSC QP?

Standard SSC QPs cover generic job roles at a sector level — useful but not tailored. When a company has proprietary equipment, unique SOPs, or a role that does not exist in any SSC catalogue, a Custom QP is needed.

MSU custom QPs are also faster to develop (3-4 months vs. 12-18 months for SSC), can be co-branded with the client, and can be used exclusively by the commissioning company before being published to the broader ecosystem.

5-Stage Custom QP Development Process

Stage 1 — Job Role Scoping (Week 1-2)

Plant/site visits by MSU curriculum team. Line manager and worker interviews. Workplace observation across shifts. Job Task Analysis (JTA) exercise covering 10-20 distinct job roles. NSQF/NCrF level determination based on complexity, autonomy, and knowledge requirements. Output: JTA Report with proposed QP scope and level.

Stage 2 — NOS and QP Drafting (Week 3-5)

National Occupational Standards (NOS) drafted for each identified task cluster. Performance criteria written at micro-task level. Knowledge and Skills delineated (K = declarative, S = procedural). Occupational description, entry requirements, progression pathways written. Client review cycle — 2 iterations with tracked changes. Output: Draft QP document in NCVET-compliant format.

Stage 3 — Curriculum and Assessment Build (Week 6-9)

Training curriculum mapped to NOS — theory hours, practical hours, OJT hours, total credit hours calculated. LMS module map created. OJT logbook template with daily/weekly observation checkpoints. Assessment rubric with weightages (theory: 30%, practical: 50%, OJT: 20% typical split). Assessor guide and calibration document. Output: Full Training Package (curriculum + assessment tools).

Stage 4 — Pilot and Validation (Week 10-12)

Pilot cohort of 10-20 learners trained and assessed using the new QP. Assessor calibration session. Feedback loop — QP revised if required. Industry expert validation panel (minimum 3 industry SMEs sign off). Output: Validated QP with pilot data and sign-off.

Stage 5 — Certification and Launch (Week 13-16)

QP registered with NCVET as MSU Awarding Body QP. Credentials designed — digital certificate template with MSU + client co-branding. DigiLocker integration configured for bulk issuance. APAAR-ABC credit registration done. NAPS portal category mapping completed. Batch API configured for scale. Output: Live QP, production-ready issuance system, first batch certified.

Frequently Asked Questions — Custom QP

How long does Custom QP development take?

Typically 3-4 months from kick-off to first certified batch. This includes site visits (Week 1-2), QP drafting and client review (Week 3-6), curriculum build (Week 6-9), pilot and validation (Week 10-12), and launch (Week 13-16). Rush track (priority) can compress to 10-12 weeks for simpler QPs.

What does it cost to develop a Custom QP?

For IP partners integrating WISE programmes, Custom QP development is included at zero additional cost — the development investment is recovered through the per-learner programme fee over the cohort lifetime. For standalone QP-only engagements, a development fee applies (quote on request).

Can we co-own the Custom QP?

Yes. MSU offers a co-ownership model where the client and MSU jointly own the QP. The client retains exclusive use rights for the agreed exclusivity period. After that, MSU may publish the QP in the national repository, giving the client first-mover advantage in having a nationally recognised occupational standard named after or aligned to their operations.

Can an existing SSC QP be customised?

Yes — MSU can adapt an existing SSC QP by adding supplementary NOS units specific to the client's operations, creating an augmented QP that is both SSC-aligned and company-specific. This hybrid approach is faster (6-8 weeks) and costs less than a fully custom QP.

Is the Custom QP NAPS eligible?

Yes. All MSU Custom QPs are designed to be NAPS-eligible. MAPL registers the QP on the Apprenticeship India Portal and maps apprenticeship trades to the QP. Learners enrolled under a Custom QP programme can avail GoI NAPS stipend subsidy (25%), EPFO PF exemption, and GST exemption on programme fees.

How are Custom QP assessors trained?

MSU trains client-side assessors (supervisors, technical leads) as Qualified Assessors via a 2-day Assessment Calibration Workshop. External assessors from MSU's assessor panel also available for independent third-party assessment. All assessors require minimum 3 years relevant industry experience and a formal assessor certification.

NCrF Framework

What is NCrF?

National Credit Framework jointly developed by MoE, MSDE, UGC, AICTE, NCVET, NCERT. Notified by UGC on 10 April 2023. Integrates School Education, Higher/Technical Education, and Vocational Education into a single credit system.

1,200 hours = 40 credits = 1 Level. 30 hours per credit. Credits earned from classroom, lab, OJT, online, apprenticeship, internship, and community work.

Level to Qualification Mapping

Level 4 (Class XII equivalent) → D.Voc, ITI, 10th+2yr work
Level 4.5 (UG Certificate) → Exit after 1yr UG / STT Certificate
Level 5 (UG Diploma) → Exit after 2yr UG / LTT Diploma
Level 5.5 (B.Voc Degree) → 3-year work-integrated degree
Level 6 (B.Tech / PGD) → 4-year engineering or PG Diploma
Level 6.5 (MBA / M.Voc) → 2-year masters
Level 8 (Doctoral) → PhD / Research programmes

Indian Army - Resettlement and re-skilling for retiring officers and JCOs/ORs
Indian Navy - Technical and management programmes for sailors and officers
Indian Air Force - Upskilling for technical trades and administrative cadre
Indian Coast Guard - Maritime logistics and safety programmes
SSB (Sashastra Seema Bal) - Border management and community skilling
Assam Rifles - Northeast-focused resettlement programmes

Resettlement Programmes: B.Voc, MBA, M.Voc for retiring personnel

Strategic Partnerships

Adani Skills - 4-Dimension Partnership Model

Dimension 1 - Academic: MSU degrees and diplomas for Adani Group employees and community members at plant locations
Dimension 2 - Vocational: NCVET/NSQF qualifications for Adani contractor workforce and local communities near industrial sites
Dimension 3 - CSR/Social: Medhavi Foundation delivers Adani Foundation CSR mandated skilling for BPL/SC/ST beneficiaries in project-affected villages
Dimension 4 - Placement: MAPL runs placement-linked programmes feeding skilled workers into Adani ports, logistics, and power operations

HDFC Bank CSR Programme

Medhavi Foundation is an HDFC Bank CSR implementation partner for skilling of rural youth in banking, insurance, and financial literacy. Programme scope: 5,000+ beneficiaries p.a. across Maharashtra, UP, and Northeast India.

Course: Banking & Financial Services (200 hrs)
Certification: MSU + HDFC jointly issued
Placement: HDFC Bank, partner NBFCs, microfinance institutions
CSR funding: Rs. 2.5 Crore p.a.

AWS re/Start Programme

Duration: 12 weeks full-time (320 hours)
Credential: AWS Cloud Practitioner + MSU Certificate
Target: Graduates from non-CS backgrounds, women returners, ESM
Placement: AWS partner companies, cloud roles in India and GCC
