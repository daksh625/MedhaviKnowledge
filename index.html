About Medhavi Skills University

Medhavi Skills University (MSU) is India's first private Skills University, established under the Medhavi Skills University Act 2021, Govt. of Chhattisgarh. UGC-recognised, NAAC-accredited, ISO 9001:2015 certified. Headquartered in Bilaspur, Chhattisgarh. Tagline: Skill · Empower · Liberate.

Design Philosophy

Captured in the phrase PERFORM · EXPERIENCE · EXPLORE · CREATE — the four learning verbs of a Skills University graduate — versus the traditional university's UNDERSTAND & MEMORISE. Employers co-design, co-deliver, assess, and fund programmes. Industry is not a downstream recipient — it is the primary architect.

Mission

To bridge the gap between education and employment by embedding learning inside industry — creating job-ready graduates who contribute from Day 1.

Vision

To be India's largest work-integrated university ecosystem, globally recognised for producing skilled, certified, and degree-qualified industry professionals.

IP — Industry Partnerships (B2B)

IP is MSU's direct engagement with corporates and employers to deliver workforce development solutions. Executed jointly by MSU (academic credibility + UGC degrees) and MAPL (operational execution + payroll + compliance). Two primary modes: Direct Corporate and Staffing.

Two Modes of IP Engagement

🏢 Direct (Corporate)

MSU partners directly with a corporate to deliver workforce skilling, credentialing, and degree programmes for their employees. Custom QPs designed around company SOPs. MSU + MAPL execute jointly — MSU provides degrees, MAPL handles NAPS/payroll.

Who: HR heads, L&D directors, CSR teams
What: WISE degrees, custom skilling, certifications, RPL
Result: Reduced attrition · Faster ramp-up · Lower cost per hire · CSR eligibility

👥 Staffing Partnership

MSU/MAPL partners with staffing firms to upskill their deployed or sourced talent, creating stickier, credential-led workforce pipelines. Associates earn degrees while working across client sites — improving stickiness, NPS, and margins for the staffing firm.

Who: Staffing companies, workforce management firms
What: WISE for deployed workforce, RPL, NAPS management
Result: Lower churn · Better client retention · Credential differentiation

🎓 WISE — Work-Integrated Degrees

Apprenticeship-embedded degree programmes (B.Voc, D.Voc, B.Sc, B.Tech, MBA) where employees study while working. RPL credits for prior experience. Blended delivery: LMS + on-floor coaching. DigiLocker-verified, ABC-registered, NSQF-mapped. Zero disruption to operations.

EP — Education Partnerships (B2B)

EP is MSU's model for external training institutes, skill centres, coaching institutes, and educational bodies seeking UGC-recognised credentials without building university infrastructure. Two sub-types: Sectoral (60+ partners) and Awarding Body (141 active partners).

What EP Partners Get

🎓 UGC-Backed Credentials

All certificates, diplomas, and degrees issued under MSU's UGC recognition. Government-backed credibility transforms partner's training into a formally recognised qualification. DigiLocker-issued and verifiable nationally.

🖥️ LMS + Exam Infrastructure

MSU's full Learning Management System provided to EP partners for course delivery, assessments, and learner tracking. MSU manages end-to-end exam design, proctoring, and result declaration — partner focuses on training.

📲 DigiLocker + ABC

All learner credentials issued via DigiLocker (India's national document repository) and registered on Academic Bank of Credits. Students own their credentials — fully portable, employer-verifiable, stackable across institutions.

🏷️ Co-Branded Certification

Certificates issued jointly under MSU + partner branding. Elevates partner's market position. Alumni carry both the partner's name and MSU's UGC recognition, improving placement outcomes and enrolment conversions.

📈 Structured Pathways

Certificate → Diploma → Degree progression aligned to NEP 2020 and NCrF. Learners can stack credits over time. RPL (Recognition of Prior Learning) enables experienced workers to skip early levels and fast-track to degrees.

⚖️ NEP 2020 Alignment

Full alignment to National Education Policy 2020 — enabling multidisciplinary learning, credit transfer, and flexible degree completion timelines. Positions partners as NEP-compliant forward-looking institutions.

Two EP Sub-Types

📚 EP Sectoral

Partnerships with sector-specific training institutes to issue MSU-recognised credentials for their vocational programmes. Institute retains its curriculum identity; MSU adds academic weight via UGC recognition, LMS, exams, and DigiLocker issuance.

Scale: 60+ active sectoral partners
Sectors: Healthcare, IT, Aviation, Beauty & Wellness, Gems, Arts, Management, CSR, Defence, Manufacturing
Degree Types: Certificate, Diploma, B.Voc, B.Sc, MBA — matched to sector & NSQF level
Best For: Training institutes that already have learners and delivery infra but lack degree power

🏛️ EP Awarding Body (A|B)

MSU acts as the pure awarding body — institute designs, markets, and delivers their programme entirely. MSU provides: LMS platform, exam infrastructure, degree/certificate issuance, DigiLocker credentials, and ABC registration. Minimal MSU involvement in delivery.

Scale: 141 active Awarding Body partners
Model: Partner is fully autonomous in curriculum & delivery; MSU is backend academic credential engine
Fee: Per-learner or fixed-fee model; partner sets their own fee to students
Best For: EdTech companies, defence/govt training units, CSR foundations, niche skill centres

First Industry Approach — 7 Principles

MSU's operating philosophy is built on the idea that industry is the primary customer of education — not a downstream recipient. Every academic decision starts with industry's need.

1. Industry-Designed Curriculum

QPs and curricula co-created with industry partners. SSC QPs as baseline; custom QPs for sector-specific needs. No academic lag. Annual refresh cycle.

2. Industry as Campus

80–90% learning at the workplace. Supervisors as mentors. OJT Logbook as primary assessment. Real output = real learning.

3. Credential Portability

DigiLocker-backed + ABC-stacked + NSQF-mapped. Learner owns credential. No institution lock-in. Internationally portable.

4. Financial Neutrality

GST-exempt, PF-exempt, NAPS-subsidised = zero incremental cost burden on industry vs. regular hiring. Plus CSR eligibility.

5. Ecosystem Neutrality

MSU works across SSCs, NSDC, UGC, MSDE, and private sector. Not captured by any single ecosystem. Widest compatibility.

6. Scale by Design

Asset-light model. LMS + partner network = infinite scale. No new campuses needed. Every partner site is an MSU campus.

7. Learner Centricity

Every decision optimised for learner employment outcomes. Earn-while-you-learn. RPL for experience. No dropout risk from financial pressure.

NCrF Framework

What is NCrF?

National Credit Framework jointly developed by MoE, MSDE, UGC, AICTE, NCVET, NCERT. Notified by UGC on 10 April 2023. Integrates School Education, Higher/Technical Education, and Vocational Education into a single credit system.

1,200 hours = 40 credits = 1 Level. 30 hours per credit. Credits earned from classroom, lab, OJT, online, apprenticeship, internship, and community work.

Level to Qualification Mapping

Level 4 (Class XII equivalent) → D.Voc, ITI, 10th+2yr work
Level 4.5 (UG Certificate) → Exit after 1yr UG / STT Certificate
Level 5 (UG Diploma) → Exit after 2yr UG / LTT Diploma
Level 5.5 (B.Voc Degree) → 3-year work-integrated degree
Level 6 (B.Tech / PGD) → 4-year engineering or PG Diploma
Level 6.5 (MBA / M.Voc) → 2-year masters
Level 8 (Doctoral) → PhD / Research programmes
